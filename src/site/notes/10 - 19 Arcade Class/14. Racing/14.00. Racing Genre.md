**Racers** center around reaching a defined objective as quickly as possible.

## Subgenres

### [[10 - 19 Arcade Class/14. Racing/14.01. Arcade Racer\|Arcade Racer]]

**Arcade Racers** feature an over-the-shoulder view of the player vehicle.
- Examples: *Out Run* (Sega, 1986); *Daytona USA* (Sega, 1994)

### [[10 - 19 Arcade Class/14. Racing/14.02. RC Racer\|RC Racer]]

**RC Racers** feature an overhead birds-eye view of the racetrack.
- Examples: *R.C. Pro-Am* (Rare, 1988); *Micro Machines* (Codemasters, 1991)

### [[10 - 19 Arcade Class/14. Racing/14.03. Kart Racer\|Kart Racer]]

**Kart Racers** are cartoonish and chaotic in nature, often featuring mascots and powerup items.
- Examples: *Super Mario Kart* (Nintendo, 1992); *Crash Team Racing* (Naughty Dog, 1999)

### [[10 - 19 Arcade Class/14. Racing/14.04. Future Racer\|Future Racer]]

**Future Racers** are amped with blazing fast speeds and heavy consequences for imprecise movement.
- Examples: *F-Zero* (Nintendo, 1990), *Wipeout* (Psygnosis, 1995)

### [[10 - 19 Arcade Class/14. Racing/14.05. Watercraft Racer\|14.05. Watercraft Racer]]

**Watercraft Racers** are like... racing but on the water and whatnot.
- Examples: *Wave Race 64* (Nintendo, 1996), *Jet Moto* (SingleTrac, 1996)

### [[10 - 19 Arcade Class/14. Racing/14.06. Winter Racer\|Winter Racer]]

**Winter Racers** feature slippery environments with snowboarding, skiing, and other winter sports.
- Examples: *1080 Snowboarding* (Nintendo, 1998); *SSX Tricky* (Electronic Arts, 2001)

### [[10 - 19 Arcade Class/14. Racing/14.07. Destruction Racer\|Destruction Racer]]

**Destruction Racers** encourage players to damage or destroy competitors. 
- Example: *Burnout* (Criterion Games, 2001)
