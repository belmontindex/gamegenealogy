**Arcade Maze** games restrict player movement to a maze, while requiring players to complete an objective and/or avoid enemies.

While [[80 - 89 Resources/81. Companies/81.04. Namco\|Namco]] found significant success in shaping the subgenre with *Pac-Man* (1980) and *Dig Dug* (1982), it was actually predated by [[80 - 89 Resources/81. Companies/81.01. Sega\|Sega's]] *Head On* (1979). 

[[80 - 89 Resources/81. Companies/81.11. Hudson Soft\|Hudson Soft's]] *Bomberman* (1983) set the stage for a Super Subgenre, the Arcade Puzzle Maze, which served as a slower-paced strategic alternative to the chase games in the style of *Pac-Man*.

The Arcade Maze subgenre would also influence the [[10 - 19 Arcade Class/14. Racing/14.02. RC Racer\|RC Racer]] subgenre through games like *Rally-X* (Namco, 1980).
