**Ball and Paddle** games have the player move along a fixed axis to bounce an object away from entering their field.

>[!warning] ***Under Construction!***

This subgenre is most commonly associated with *Pong* ([[80 - 89 Resources/81. Companies/81.03. Atari\|Atari]], 1972) and its innumerable clones at the time. However, occasional arcade releases built on the formula through the 1990s.

<video src="https://dillonbelmont.com/02genealogy-assets/10/ballpaddle.mp4" controls width="100%"></video>
*Battle Flip Shot (Visco, 1998)*

The subgenre saw some resurgence in popularity due to sustained competitive interest in *Windjammers* (Data East, 1994), which even received a modern sequel in 2022. Ball and Paddle mechanics may also commonly be found in the [[30 - 39 Social Class/32. Sports/32.00. Sports Genre#Compilation\|Sports Compilation]] subgenre in the form of Air Hockey or similar activities.

Notably, Ball and Paddle games were also responsible for the [[10 - 19 Arcade Class/11. Arcade/11.02. Block Breaker\|Block Breaker]] subgenre. *Clean Sweep* (Ramtek, 1974), and later *Breakout* (Atari, 1976), flipped the player axis on its side and removed multiplayer to shift the gameplay objective away from competition.
